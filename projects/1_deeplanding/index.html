Active Colloids in Shear Flow

Master's Thesis

Duration: May’18 - June’19

Supervisor: Prof. Rahul Mangal

Artificial active janus particles (JPs) have recently gained a lot of interest because of their ability to mimic transport behaviour of various living microorganisms and also because of their potential to be used as drug delivery agents. To fully harness their potential, investigation of their response to various external stimuli including external flow is of great interest. Therefore, in this study, we perform experiments on silica-platinum Janus particles suspended in a capillary in an aqueous medium with varying hydrogen peroxide (fuel) concentrations at different shear flow rates. A transition in the motion of JPs from the usual random active motion to migration across stream lines and then finally to migration along the flow, was observed. Our analysis revealed that these transitions were dictated by the relative time scale of rotation of Janus particles due to propulsion near wall w.r.t. shear induced rotation time scale. Interestingly, we found that when these time scales are comparable, particles align in a manner such that they migrate across streamlines. This behaviour however, was not prevalent at very slow and at very high flows.
